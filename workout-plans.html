Build.

Perfect for anyone looking to build some muscle, this training programs follows the popular bodybuilding Push-Pull-Leg (PPL) split. Designed to maximize muscle growth and strength, this program will take you through a balanced mix of compound lifts and isolation exercises to target every major muscle group. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced lifter, this program can be tailored to your fitness level and be done 3 or 6 days a week.

Photo from StrengthLog

What You'll Need










Before You Begin...

  1. If you are unfamiliar with any of the exercises, especially if you are a beginner, look through our tips and tutorial videos.
  2. For beginners, start with doing this workout 3 days a week, with a day of rest between sessions.

    Advanced lifters can increase the frequency to six days a week with one rest day at the end or follow a non-weekly cycle of three days on, one day off.

Run.

A well-rounded running plan built to improve your speed, stamina, and strength in just three days a week. With a mix of intervals, steady runs, and supportive strength training, this program helps you run faster, feel lighter, and stay injury-free no matter your current pace.

Plan Overview

Day Focus Goal
1 Intervals / Speed Build speed and power
2 Strength and Core Support running, prevent injury
3 Long / Steady Run Improve endurance and fat burn

The Workout

Day 1: Intervals / Speed
Warm-up (5-10 mins)
  • Light jog + dynamic stretches
  • High knees, butt kicks, leg swings
Speed Intervals
  • Option A: 8 x 200m sprints (fast effort) with 60s walk in between
  • Option B: 30s sprint / 90s walk x 6-8 rounds
  • Option C: Hill sprints - 6 x 20-30s sprint, walk back down
Cool Down
  • Easy jog + static stretches
Day 2: Strength + Core
Lower Body
  • Goblet Squats x 10
  • Reverse Lunges x 10 per leg
  • Romanian Deadlifts x 10
  • Calf Raises x 15
  • Glute Bridges x 12
Core Finisher
  • Plank x 1 min
  • Bicycle Crunches x 30
  • V-ups x 15
  • Side Plank x 30s per side
Day 3: Steady Run
  • Steady State Run (30-40 mins): Moderate, consistent pace
  • Focus on pacing, relaxed breathing, and gradual distance/time increase each week

Ten Tips For Exercise Beginners

Article from Everyone Active by Steph Heath

  1. Set Realistic Goals
    It may take some time to get the results you want. It’s vital to break your ultimate goal down in to stages and set lots of smaller goals to keep you motivated along the way.
  2. Be Consistent
    Results come through regular and consistent activity. Stick to your programme and avoid frequent stops and starts.
  3. Too Much Too Soon
    Do not increase the amount of exercise you do too soon. Increase what you’re doing by no more than 10% per week.
  4. Accept Feelings of Discomfort
    When you first start exercising you’ll experience feelings of discomfort such as shortness of breath, sweating and aching muscles after exercising. Don’t worry, this is completely normal and everyone feels like this when they’re first starting out.
  5. Train With a Friend
    Training with a friend not only keeps you motivated during the sessions themselves but will also make you less likely to miss a planned session as you’re unlikely to want to let down your friend.
  6. Warm Up
    Not warming up is a mistake many beginners make. Before any session ensure that you warm up thoroughly and mobilise the areas that you will be using during the workout. Furthermore, failure to warm up properly may increase the risk of injury.
  7. Time of Day
    We are all different. Some of us rise early and some of us go to bed late. Exercise at the time when you feel that you have most energy.
  8. Will Power
    Don’t give up. All beginners have set backs on the way to achieving their goals. Accept them and use them to re-motivate yourself.
  9. Water
    Ensure you drink plenty of water, before, during and after exercise, particularly if you are exercising in warm conditions or for long periods. Dehydration will result in a drop in performance and severe dehydration can be dangerous too.
  10. Try Something New
    Keep things interesting by trying new exercises, workouts and activities to keep your programme fresh, keep challenging your body and preventing boredom.
