Let's Talk Protein

By now, we've all heard about the high-protein diets for optimal muscle-building and even for general health and well being. We've started adding protein powder to our yogurt, oats, coffee, shakes, desserts, and pretty much everything else we can imagine. While, it's good to be getting more protein, we need to conscious of the amount and the quality of the protein we're getting.

  The debate on how much protein you need for muscle-building has not yet been settled. While the recommended dietary allowance (RDA) for protein is 0.8 g per kg of body-weight, many body-building sources will tell you to have at least 2 grams per kilogram of bodyweight. With all these varying answers, a good target to aim for would be somewhere in between. Medical News Today (2022) states that: the optimum amount of protein for muscle-building appears to be between 1.2 and 1.6 g per kg of body weight.

It is also important to prioritise whole food protein sources and only use supplements to help fill the gaps. Whole food protein is absorbed more slowly and used more efficiently by our bodies, keeping us feeling full and energised for longer.
